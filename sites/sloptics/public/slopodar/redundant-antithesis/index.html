Redundant Antithesis

detected 2026-02-27

trigger

"caught in the wild — not theorised in advance"

what it is

Negative-positive antithesis where the negation adds zero information. "Caught in the wild" already implies "not theorised." The negation is dead weight. The classical form ("not A, but B") is a deliberate rhetorical choice; the LLM form is an RLHF-trained reflex.

what it signals

instead

Just say the positive. "Entries are added when caught in the wild." If the contrast genuinely adds meaning, keep it. If the reader already knows the negated term, cut it.
